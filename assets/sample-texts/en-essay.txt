Typography is the craft of giving language a visible body. The shape a text takes on the page is not the message itself, yet it carries an overwhelming amount of non-verbal information: the sensibility of an age, the memory of history, the trace of a hand. In the instant before reading begins, in the turning of a page, typography meets the reader and sets up expectation. Good typography is largely invisible; the better it is done, the less the reader notices it.
